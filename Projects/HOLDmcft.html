(MCFT) 03-05-2021: Maintain Hold Rating due to Slowing Demand and Boat Show Cancellations

I am maintaining my HOLD recommendation for Master Craft Boat Holdings with a price target of $32 which is 18.5% from the current price of $27.33 as of market close 03/04/2021.

Slowing Demand

Demand has slowed recently due to the winter storms and polar vortex that the southern markets of Texas, Louisiana, and Florida panhandle experienced. Most dealers should start to see business pick up again and the midwest has started their spring sales again. It is more likely that demand will not start to recover for another week or two as we see warmer weather and "spring fever" pursuade consumers to get a boat. In addition, improving economic outlook and fears of rising interest rates might lead consumers to purchase and finance a boat now rather than wait until later and risk higher interest rates.

Popular Midwest Boat Shows Cancelled

One highly concenctrated and popular market for MCFT is the midwest and one main attraction for midwesterners is visiting boat shows in the cold winter offseason. However, many boat shows have been cancelled this year due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The Chicagoland Fishing Travel & Outdoor Expo that usually takes place in late February or early March was moved to 2022. I personnaly attend this show every year to explore new types of products and equipment and with no virtual version this will be a loss of opportunity for many companies. The Northern Illinois Boat Show organizers followed the same guidance as the Schaumburg Show by moving their show to next year.

These boat shows are a great way for MCFT and similar companies to promote their brand new lines of products and get pre-orders. It also drives interest for all consumers that attend that may not even want a boat but are willing to try new consumer activities. With the shows being cancelled this will hurt MCFT and similar companies, however, I do not believe this to be a material impact on sales or the bottom line. It also provides an opportunity for MCFT to capitalize on: shows may be cancelled but the consumers are still looking to spend. It will be interesting to see if MCFT acts on this opportunity and targets these new customers.
