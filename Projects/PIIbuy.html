(PII) 12-14-2020: Upper Peninsula Trip Confirms Supply Chain Issues

I am issuing a Buy Recommendation for Polaris with a price target of $115 which is 24.9% from the current price of $92.07 as of market close 10/27/2020. My recommendation is based on greater demand for PII's products than consensus expects.

Demand exceeds consensus expectations

In the early spring, when COVID was ravaging the economy and uncertainty was high I was still spending my days fishing. I immediately started to notice the increase in fishing pressure from both local fisherman and people who have never fished before. I made the realization that COVID will introduce so many new people to fishing and the outdoors, in general. I made the early investment into PII, MCFT, and MBUU with expectations that sales will be boosted by the pandemic rather than hurt by it. I was right.

However, I am issuing a new recommendation for PII because I believe the same situation will occur with winter sports and activities. My understanding of consensus thinking has also led me to believe that consensus is not viewing the demand increase as high as I have seen it happen. My friends, fishing buddies and their families have migrated to their lake house and cabins in the summer and plan to do the same in the fall, especially as COVID cases are starting to rise again. My contacts at outdoors shops have already indicated that preorders are starting to diminish stock. This is what my thesis is built around.

Some other aspects to note. PII has released their new models for 2021, with no significant changes but a product offering that exceeds 800 types of vehicles, equipment, parts, etc. Guidance is strong but again not as excited as I am for the winter season to boost sales.

The only risk that I currently see is a supply chain problem. Management has mentioned this before, expecially with their boat product line in the summer but suppliers are having issues meeting distributor demand. The same may occur for snowmobiles and ORV's in the winter season as consumer demand explodes and supply can not keep up. This will be something to keep an eye on.
